About.

Hi, I'm Jared, a UCLA freshman born and raised in Los Angeles, California (No, I don't see celebrities everyday). My humble journey as a programmer began my sophomore year of high school where I wrote my first program with Scratch, a language widely used by eight year olds learning to code. Since then, my skills have matured to include more advanced languages that are all listed in my resume (I didn't want to bore you here by naming them all). When I'm not staring at my computer screen, you can find me practicing calisthenics tricks at Drake Stadium, lifeguarding at the UCLA pools, or going out for late night food runs. Scroll further down to see more about me!

Calisthenics

I'm a part of the Bruin Street Workout Club at UCLA. We meet weekly at Drake Stadium to work out and practice calisthenics tricks. Our club also participates in competitions against other schools' calisthenics clubs. Right now, I'm training to do a handstand push up and eventually a one arm handstand.

Lifeguarding

I started working as a lifeguard my freshman year of college. I’ve always been used to being in the water from playing water polo and swimming all of high school. As a lifeguard, I’m CPR/AED certified and trained in First Aid. I mainly work for UCLA Rercreation and Aquatics, but I also do private lifeguarding with Happy Swimmers USA.
